# Interview Questions

## 1. What are duplicate records?

**Answer:**

Duplicate records are rows or entries that appear more than once in a dataset.

---

## 2. Why is removing duplicates important?

**Answer:**

Removing duplicates improves data accuracy, prevents double counting, and ensures reliable analysis.

---

## 3. Which Excel tab contains the Remove Duplicates feature?

**Answer:**

The **Data** tab.

---

## 4. Can Excel remove duplicates based on multiple columns?

**Answer:**

Yes. Excel allows you to select one or more columns when checking for duplicate records.

---

## 5. What should you do before removing duplicates?

**Answer:**

Create a backup of the dataset and verify which columns should be used to identify duplicates.

---

## 1. What are missing values?

**Answer:**

Missing values are blank or empty cells where data is unavailable or not recorded.

---

## 2. Why should missing values be handled?

**Answer:**

Because they can reduce data accuracy, affect formulas, distort analysis, and lead to incorrect business decisions.

---

## 3. How can you find blank cells in Excel?

**Answer:**

- Filter → **(Blanks)**
- Go To Special → **Blanks**
- Conditional Formatting

---

## 4. Should missing values always be deleted?

**Answer:**

No. They should only be deleted if the missing information makes the record unusable. Otherwise, they can be replaced with an appropriate value or left blank if suitable.

---

## 5. What should you do before modifying missing values?

**Answer:**

Create a backup of the original dataset and understand why the values are missing.

---


## 1. What is data standardization?

**Answer:**

Data standardization is the process of making data consistent by applying the same format across the entire dataset.

---

## 2. Why is standardization important?

**Answer:**

It improves data quality, ensures consistency, reduces errors, and makes analysis more reliable.
